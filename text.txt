wide rice noodles:

Soak noodles: Place 2-3 ounces (or about 60-90 grams) of wide rice noodles in a large bowl and cover them with warm water. Let the noodles soak for about 15-20 minutes or until they are soft and pliable.

Boil water: Fill a medium-sized pot with about 2-3 cups of water and bring it to a rolling boil.

Cook noodles: Once the water is boiling, add the soaked noodles to the pot and stir occasionally to prevent them from sticking together. Cook the noodles for about 2-3 minutes or until they are tender. Do not overcook the noodles, as they can become mushy.

Drain noodles: Once the noodles are cooked, drain them using a colander or a strainer. Rinse the noodles under cold water to stop the cooking process and to remove any excess starch.

Serve noodles: Serve the noodles hot with your favorite sauce or toppings. You can also toss the noodles in a little oil or butter to keep them from sticking together.

egg noodles:

Boil water: Fill a medium-sized pot with about 2-3 cups of water and bring it to a rolling boil.

Add salt: Once the water is boiling, add a pinch of salt to it.

Cook noodles: Add 2-3 ounces (or about 60-90 grams) of egg noodles to the boiling water and stir occasionally to prevent them from sticking together. Cook the noodles for about 3-5 minutes or until they are al dente.

Drain noodles: Once the noodles are cooked, drain them using a colander or a strainer. Rinse the noodles under cold water to stop the cooking process and to remove any excess starch.

Serve noodles: Serve the noodles hot with your favorite sauce or toppings. You can also toss the noodles in a little oil or butter to keep them from sticking together.

udon noodles:

Serve the noodles hot with your favorite sauce or broth, or add them to stir-fry dishes.


------
whole grain noodles


Boil a large pot of water and add the whole grain noodles. Cook for about 7-8 minutes or until they are tender.

Drain the noodles and rinse them under cold water to stop the cooking process.

Serve the noodles hot with your favorite sauce or broth, or add them to stir-fry dishes. Note that whole grain noodles have a nuttier flavor and firmer texture compared to traditional noodles.
